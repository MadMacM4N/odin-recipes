Sweet Crepe Recipe

This recipe is a crowd favorite, and credited to Alton Brown.

With a list of ingredients found in most pantries they can be

made without a trip to the grocer. Prep time is short, and a

minimum resting period of 60 minutes means it can be

ready quickly. To save time in the morning batter can be

prepared the night before. The example shown used fresh

blueberries, strawberries. Many items pair well, such as

jams and spreads, fruit, syrup, powdwered or cinnamon-sugar

Ingredients:

  • 2 Large Eggs
  • 3/4 Cup Milk
  • 1/2 Cup Water
  • 1 Cup AP Flour
  • 3 Tbsp. Butter
  • 3 Tbsp. Sugar
  • 1 tsp. Vanilla

Steps:

  1. In a blender, combine all of the ingredients and pulse for 10 seconds.
  2. Place the crepe batter in the refrigerator for one hour (max 48h)
  3. Heat a small non-stick pan over med-low heat, add butter to coat.
  4. Pour 1 oz. of batter into center of pan and swirl to spread evenly.
  5. Cook for 30-60 seconds or until surface loses shine and edges begin to brown.
  6. Flip and cook for another 30 seconds.
  7. Lay them out flat to cool.
  8. When cool, stack and pack in an airtight refrigerated container for several days.
  9. Frozen crepes will keep for up to 2 months.

Notes:

The resting period can be shortened but will increase the amount of bubbles in the batter
